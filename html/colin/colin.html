The Old Man and the Sea

Book by Ernest Hemingway, Review by Colin Chevalier

The Old Man and the Sea is a short novel about an aging Cuban fisherman named Santiago, and his struggles with aging described through his pursuit of a massive marlin.
Through his struggles of trying to reel in the mosnter fish, he battles with ocean life, nature, and himself.

This is one of my favorite books, and my most frequently recommended book when people tell me they would like to get back into reading.
It is a short novel at only 127 pages, and easily digestable with points to think on long after one has finished reading it. I enjoy the symbolism of Santiago's age being
both a benefit and hinderance to his pursuit of the marlin- his aging body makes the physical strains more difficult but his wisdom and experience help him.
To me, it is his last act of youth.
